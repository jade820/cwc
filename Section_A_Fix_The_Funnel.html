The picture

Where the money is leaking

The analysis

Three patterns across both calls

Same story in the Jess call and the Jalisa call. Both got a brilliant, complete plan. Neither got an offer.

1

The offer never actually gets made

In both calls you closed with a roadmap and a soft "we can talk about support if you want." No specific package, no price, no clear next step, no ask. The call just ends warmly.

Your close with Jess

"I'll have everything by the end of the week... if you're looking for more support, we can talk about what that looks like with retainer structure."

Your close with Jalisa

"I will have for you on Monday a full 90-day roadmap... tap me when you get to that stage."

The fixEnd every call with one specific offer, one price, and one next step. Say the number out loud on the call.
2

You over-deliver, so there is nothing left to buy

Both women walked away with the full transformation for free: content pillars, power partner strategy, batching systems, the works. You closed the gap that a retainer is supposed to fill. Jess was ready to take it and run.

Jess, mid-call

"I love the way that you think... there's so much potential, but I need the systems."

Luke's word for it

"Is 90 minutes too open-ended? Does it feel like free consulting?"

The fixGive them the what and the why on the call. Position the how, the done-with-you execution, as the retainer.
3

One format for everyone, no matter where they are

Jess is high intent with a $6,500 program of her own and a clear systems need. Jalisa is early stage, overwhelmed, and finding her footing. Both got the same deep-strategy call. High-intent Jess was never closed, and early-stage Jalisa was never handed a right-sized entry point.

Jalisa's core need

"How to sell myself, how to put myself out there... attracting clients is the biggest one of all."

Jess's core need

"Inbound leads, owned ecosystem, repeatable revenue... I'm lacking the systems and operations for sure."

The fixQualify before the call, then match the person to the right offer. Not everyone gets the same door (see Section A, part 3).
The diagnosis

Is it the offer, the price, the timing, or the format?

Luke asked you to name the real culprit. Here it is. It is not the price, because nobody ever got to a price.

Structure
Primary cause

The call has no close built into it. It ends on a roadmap, not an ask.

The offer
Primary cause

There is no defined offer to make. It needs to exist before the call starts.

Timing
Secondary

The moment is fine. The ask simply never happens, so timing cannot land.

Price
Not the issue yet

Prospects never reach a number, so price cannot be what is stopping them.

Bottom line: This is a structure and offer problem, not a lead problem or a pricing problem. Build a real offer, build a close into the call, and the same calls you are already having start converting.
The rebuild

Your new 90-minute call structure

Four phases, same warmth you already bring, with one difference: the close is now a real part of the call, not an afterthought.

10 min
Opening

Reconnect and set the frame

Warm catch-up, then set expectations: "We'll map your priorities today, and at the end I'll show you exactly how we can keep working together if it feels like a fit." You have now earned the right to make an offer later.

25 min
Discovery

Their world, in their words

Your business audit questions, the three pillars, the "what drains your battery" prompt. Listen for the one problem they keep circling back to. That problem is what your offer solves.

40 min
Value

Give them the map, not the whole build

Show them the strategy and the what and the why so they get a real win. Hold back the done-with-you execution. When you feel the urge to give away the full system, that is your signal that a retainer belongs here instead.

15 min
The close
The part that was missing

Make the specific offer

"Here is what I'd recommend as your next step, here is what it includes, and here is the investment." Name the package. Name the price. Name the start. Then stop talking and let them answer.

The one habit change: record and transcribe your next call, and analyze it the same day with the prompt from your checklist. You will see the leak in real time and fix it faster than any strategy can.
The offer

The exact thing you say at the close

Two doors, so you always have a right-sized next step no matter who is in front of you. Pick the door based on how they qualified. Prices are yours to set as you test, so slot your numbers in.

For higher-intent, like Jess

The Foundation Retainer

Ready now, has budget, clear pain point
  • Three-month engagement, done with you
  • The system built and implemented, not just mapped
  • Weekly touchpoints and priority Slack access
  • Executive assistant placement through Camryn where it fits
The ask: "Sign this week and your fourth month is on us." A reason to decide on the call, not later.
For earlier-stage, like Jalisa

The Momentum Starter

Motivated but not ready for the full retainer
  • A focused entry offer at a lower commitment
  • The 90-minute roadmap plus a set number of follow-ups
  • Slack support add-on to stay close between steps
  • A clear upgrade path into the Foundation Retainer
The ask: "Let's get you started here, and when you outgrow it, we move you up." No one leaves empty-handed.
Qualify first

The post-podcast qualifying survey

Four quick questions, camera off, right after you finish recording. Genuine curiosity, not a pitch. Each one maps to one of your four service areas and scores 1 to 10.

Ask warmly: "Before we wrap, do you mind if I ask you three or four quick things about where your business is right now?" Score each answer 1 (totally handled) to 10 (biggest headache). The highest scores tell you which workshop and offer to route them toward.
Routes to → Internal Systems (Camryn)
When you think about the behind-the-scenes of your business, your operations, tools, and handoffs, how smooth does it feel?High score = systems are a mess and a stress

Routes to → Community Growth (core CWC)
How strong is the community around your brand, people who would show up, refer, and buy without you chasing them?High score = relying on cold outreach, no warm base

Then tag and route

Any 7 to 10→Tag them by their highest area and invite them to that workshop as a guest. A warm, specific, free next step.
Multiple 7+→High fit across areas. This is your 90-minute strategy call shortlist and your best retainer prospects.
All under 5→Not a fit right now. Keep them warm in the community, no pitch needed.
Close with consent: "Would it be okay if I follow up with a free workshop invite that fits what you just shared?" A yes puts them straight into the right list.
